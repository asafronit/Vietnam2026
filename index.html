Practical Tips
  • Train Street: officially restricted — entry via a café (drink minimum); check it’s open that day; stand behind the line when the train passes

▶ Ha Giang Loop — Vietnam Most Insane Road Trip
Practical Tips
  • Ha Giang permit: foreigners need a border-area travel permit (~$10) — confirm the agent/tour arranges it and carry a copy
  • Nho Que boat: optional & extra (~$5–7pp), buy at the pier; the descent to the jetty is steep — agree any xe-om price first
  • Ma Pi Leng: the viewpoints are free — don’t pay touts for “entry”; only the clifftop Panorama café charges
  • Lung Cu Flag Tower: ~389 steps (electric buggy for a small fee); coldest, most exposed point — layer up
  • Homestay dinners: hosts offer “happy water” (corn rice wine) — pace yourself; bring cash, facilities are basic
  • Easy-rider / scooter: ride pillion with the guide unless you hold a valid licence — police checkpoints on the loop and insurance is void without one

▶ Solo Cycling Adventure — Ninh Binh Vietnam
Practical Tips
  • Mua Cave: ~500 uneven stone steps — go early (before 08:00) for cool air, fewer crowds & the best light; grippy shoes
  • Bai Dinh is huge — take the electric buggy between gates (small fee), allow 2–3 hrs; dress modestly (shoulders/knees) for the pagodas
  • Trang An boat: ~3 hrs, low cave ceilings (duck); bring sun cover & a small tip for the rower — pick the route with the “Kong: Skull Island” set
  • Don’t confuse Trang An (sampan, temples) with Tam Coc (shorter, rice fields) — the agent day is Trang An
  • Pack for the night train before dinner Day 13; soft-sleeper berths, lock luggage
